
Feature Engineering Summary

This benchmark uses all five challenge datasets. Daily Baltic Dry Index data is transformed into monthly features beyond a simple mean: level, volatility, monthly range, last observed value, intra-month return, one-month momentum, high-low spread, trading-day count, and spike-day count. This preserves information about shipping stress intensity and instability that would be lost under naive monthly averaging.

Brent crude oil is included as an international fuel and transport-cost driver. Botswana policy rate is included as a domestic monetary policy response variable, expected to affect inflation with longer lags. FAO Botswana variables provide the target and local price-index context. The HCP cross-country file is used to add South Africa, Namibia, Kenya, and Zimbabwe inflation features, reflecting regional trade exposure and possible spillovers into Botswana food prices.

The forecast window is January 2024 to December 2024. No actual 2024 values are used. Future exogenous features are generated using a simple leakage-safe seasonal-naive projection inside the recursive forecast routine, while target lags are updated recursively from model predictions.
